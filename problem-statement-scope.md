# Problem Statement
Many universities manage internship activities using manual and paper-based process. Students submit weekly reports in hard copy, which can be delayed, misplaced or lost. Supervisors review reports physically, making it difficult to track progress efficiently. Academic supervisors lack real-time visibility into student activities, leading to delayed evaluations and inconsistent performance tracking.
There is no centralized digital system to manage internship placements, weekly report submissions, supervisor feedback, performance scoring and institutional reporting.
Therefore, a structured, workflow-driven digital platform is required to improve transparency, efficiency, accountability, and real-time monitoring of internship activities.

## Problem Scope
The Internship Logging and Evaluation System (ILES) is a web- based platform designed to digitize and manage internship processes within an academic institution.
The system will:
•	Allow students to submit weekly internship reports online.
•	Enable company supervisors to review reports and provide feedback.
•	Allow academic supervisors to evaluate performance and assign final grades.
•	Manage internship placements (student-company-linkage).
•	Implement workflow states (Draft – Submitted – Reviewed – Evaluated).
•	Automatically compute weighted performance scores.
•	Provide administrative dashboards for monitoring progress.
•	Enforce role-based access control (Student, Supervisor, Admin).

The System will NOT:
•	Handle internship recruitment or job matching.
•	Manage payments or stipends.
•	Replace official university grading systems.
•	Include a mobile application (Version 1 is web-based only).
